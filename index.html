Nikolas Melissaris

Hey. I'm Nikolas. I come from Papagou, a small suburb outside of Athens, Greece. Nice of you to stop by. I like mathematics, especially the kind that can be applied to computer science. I'm a PhD student at Rutgers University where I'm lucky to have Periklis Papakonstantinou and Jaideep Vaidya as my advisors. My research interests include complexity theory, cryptography, computer security, and privacy.

In the past, I graduated from the School of Applied Mathematical and Physical Sciences at the National Technical University of Athens and I did PhD-level coursework at CUNY before transferring. I've worked at Nessos, the Computer Security Lab at UCSB, and Columbia University. I've taught about discrete mathematics, computer applications, and web programming,

I live in the wonderful town of Westfield in New Jersey.

It is my hope that one day we'll create the technology to be able to vote on important governamental decisions from the comfort of our home, bringing society one step closer to true, direct democracy. I dislike illogical discussions that lead nowhere, and reasoning based on faith, not evidence.
